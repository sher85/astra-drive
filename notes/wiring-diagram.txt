Stepper Motor Wiring (28BYJ-48):
- IN1 → D8 (Arduino pin 8)
- IN2 → D9 (Arduino pin 9)
- IN3 → D10 (Arduino pin 10)
- IN4 → D11 (Arduino pin 11)
- VCC (Red wire) → 5V power supply
- GND (Blue wire) → Ground

Button Wiring:
- One leg → D2 (Arduino pin 2) via jumper connected to D12 on breadboard
- Opposite leg → GND (Ground rail on breadboard) via jumper connected to D14
- Button straddles columns D and G at rows 12 and 14

Power:
- Arduino can be powered via USB or 5V battery pack
- Stepper motor should ideally use a separate 5V power supply for reliability

Notes:
- Button uses internal pull-up resistor (configured in code)
- Motor driven with Stepper library at adjustable RPM
- Confirm jumper wire orientation and breadboard rows
- LED lights up solid when motor is running (connected to D3)
- Ensure LED and resistor are on the same side of the breadboard center gap or bridged properly
